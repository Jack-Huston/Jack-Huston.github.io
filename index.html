About Me

I'm an aerospace engineer who likes problems that sit at the intersection of simulation, software, and real-world data. In my current role at Lockheed Martin Space, I support hypergolic propulsion systems across testing, anomaly/root-cause work, and analysis automation.

I’m currently pursuing an M.S. in Aerospace Engineering (Professional M.S., remote/asynchronous) with a focus on autonomous systems. I’m targeting GN&C roles in Seattle, WA, and I’m especially interested in teams working on flight software, autonomy, and mission-critical simulation.

In parallel with my coursework, I’m working toward a transition into Guidance, Navigation & Control (GN&C) and autonomous systems, with a long-term goal of working in camera-based vision for navigation and landing. I enjoy building simulations and test cases to check that estimation and control algorithms behave well in demanding situations.
