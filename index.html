About Me

I'm an aerospace engineer who likes problems at the intersection of simulation, software, and real-world data. In my current role at Lockheed Martin Space, I support hypergolic propulsion systems across testing, anomaly investigations, and analysis automation.

I’m pursuing an M.S. in Aerospace Engineering (Professional M.S., remote/asynchronous) with a focus on autonomous systems. I’m targeting GN&C roles in Seattle, WA. I’m most interested in teams working on flight software and simulation.

In parallel with my coursework, I’m shifting toward Guidance, Navigation & Control (GN&C) and autonomous systems. My long-term goal is to work on camera-based vision systems for space vehicle navigation, descent, and landing.
